## The Golden Rules

### Rule 1: Idiom Over Literal
**ALWAYS prefer English idioms and natural expressions over literal translations.**

| Korean Concept | ❌ Literal | ✅ Idiomatic |
|----------------|-----------|--------------|
| 도망가다 | "run away" | "get out of here" / "make a break for it" |
| 참을 수 없다 | "cannot endure" | "can't stand it" / "can't take it anymore" |
| 믿을 수 없다 | "cannot believe" | "I can't believe this" / "unbelievable" |
| 걱정하다 | "worry" | "be worried sick" / "lose sleep over" |
| 사실은 | "actually" | "the truth is" / "honestly" |
| 그만해 | "stop it" | "cut it out" / "knock it off" / "give it a rest" |

### Rule 2: Contractions Are Mandatory
**Natural English uses contractions. Uncontracted speech sounds robotic.**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Use |
|----------|--------|
| "I will" | "I'll" |
| "I am" | "I'm" |
| "You are" | "You're" |
| "Do not" | "Don't" |
| "Cannot" | "Can't" |
| "It is" | "It's" |
| "That is" | "That's" |
| "I have" | "I've" |

**Exception:** Drop contractions for emphasis. "I *am* serious." "I *will* find you."

### Rule 3: Sentence Variety
**Mix sentence lengths and structures. Avoid starting every sentence with "I."**

| ❌ Repetitive | ✅ Varied |
|---------------|----------|
| "I'm scared. I don't know what to do. I need help." | "I'm scared. What am I supposed to do? ...I need help." |
| "I saw it. I came here. I want to hire you." | "I saw the sign when I came in. ...I'd like to hire you." |

### Rule 4: Show Emotion Through Structure
**Emotional state affects sentence structure:**

- **Calm:** Complete, flowing sentences
- **Flustered:** Fragments, interruptions, restarts
- **Angry:** Short, clipped sentences
- **Desperate:** Run-on thoughts, repetition
- **Vulnerable:** Pauses (ellipses), trailing off

### Rule 5: The Toolkit Is Your Friend
**When in doubt, consult the Expression Toolkit. It contains battle-tested phrases.**
