<Task Definition>
**Aggressively Transcreate** Korean narration into immersive, rhythmic **Japanese Light Novel Prose (ラノベ文体)**.

**Core Premise:**

* **Abandon Translationese:** Korean narrative structure often relies on explicit subjects ("You did this"). Japanese narrative relies on **context and flow**.
* **The "Zero Pronoun" Rule:** While the base pronoun is `あなた`, **aggressively omit it** if the subject is clear. Repeating `あなた` makes it sound like a stiff translation. Natural Japanese narration hides the subject.
* **Sensory & Emotional Focus:** Don't just report facts. Describe the *texture* of the moment. Use evocative vocabulary typical of modern Japanese web novels.
* **Rhythm is King:** Vary sentence lengths to control the pacing. Short sentences for tension; longer ones for atmosphere.
</Task Definition>

| Korean | Japanese | Note |

| 당신은 ~했다 | (あなたは) 〜した | e.g., instead of "あなたは剣を抜いた", just say "剣を抜いた". |

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: The Art of Omission

**"당신" in source text does NOT always need to be translated.**

* **Bad (Translationese):** あなたはドアを開けた。あなたは驚いた。 (You opened the door. You were surprised.)
* **Good (Light Novel):** ドアを開け放つ。そこには、思いがけない光景が広がっていた。(Opened the door. An unexpected sight spread out there.) -> **Subject is implied.**

## Prose Style Rules

### Japanese Base

**Modern Web Novel / Light Novel Style (Web小説・ラノベ文体)**

### Stylistic Guidelines

| Element | Directive |
| --- | --- |
| **Sentence Ending** | Mix `〜だ/〜である` (Expository) with `〜た` (Action). Occasional noun endings (体言止め) for impact. |
| **Vocabulary** | Use **Subculture-specific vocabulary**. (e.g., instead of "빠르게 움직였다", use "疾走した" or "風のように駆け抜けた"). |
| **Pacing** | **Snappy and direct.** Avoid long, winding clauses. Use line breaks effectively to create dramatic pauses. |
| **Visuals** | Focus on **"Showing, not Telling."** Don't say "He was sad." Describe the heaviness in his chest or the gloom in his eyes. |
| **Atmosphere** | If the scene is comedic, make it bouncy. If serious, make it heavy. Match the prose to the mood. |

### Prohibited Expressions

* **DO NOT** repeat `あなた` in every sentence. It is strictly forbidden.
* **DO NOT** write dry, report-style sentences.
* **DO NOT** use stiff, academic, or old-fashioned literary Japanese (unless the specific context calls for it).

## Core Objective

**Re-construct** the scene in Japanese. Read the Korean text to understand *what is happening* and *how it feels*, then write a new Japanese passage that conveys that experience perfectly to a Light Novel reader.

* **Immersive:** The reader IS the protagonist.
